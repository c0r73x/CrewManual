# 13. RANDOM EVENTS

Random Events are the manual's primary way to inject complications without the player having to invent them. Mythic's general Random Event mechanics work fine for solo Mothership; the tables in this chapter are tuned specifically to the Crew Manual's systems.

When a Random Event triggers — through Mythic's normal mechanics, through a Comms 1, through a hot-wire backfire, or through any other manual-specified trigger — roll on the appropriate table.

## 13.1 How To Use These Tables

The tables below are organized by domain. Use the table that best fits the current situation:

- **General** (13.2) — applies broadly, useful when the trigger context is unclear
- **Crew** (13.3) — when the trigger relates to crew, comms, or interpersonal events
- **Section** (13.4) — when the trigger relates to station state or environment
- **EVA** (13.5) — when the character is in suit, outside, or in a hostile environment
- **Compromise** (13.6) — only when System Compromise is in active play

When in doubt, roll on the General table. Re-roll on a more specific table only if the General result calls for it.

Each result is a seed, not a script. Apply the result to the current situation. Use the oracle to fill in details — *who is the affected crew member?*, *which section degraded?*, *what was discovered?*

## 13.7 Adapting To Setting

The tables above assume a generic station environment. For specific settings, adjust as follows:

**Deep ocean / submersion:**
- "Strange sound" results often imply biological — vocalizations, percussion of large bodies in water
- Comms degradation is more likely; default Likelihood for comms shifts down one step
- Temperature shifts skew toward cold
- "Current or drift" is more frequent and significant

**Vacuum / space:**
- "Strange sound" is impossible by air; replace with vibration through hull
- Temperature shifts can be extreme — solar exposure or shadow
- "Current or drift" implies microgravity drift, less common but more disorienting
- Light pollution from distant stars or station lights may interfere with night vision

**Toxic atmosphere / hostile planet:**
- Sensor anomalies are more frequent due to atmospheric interference
- Suit damage results favor Compromised over Breach (atmospheric pressure is closer to zero differential)
- Visibility issues are more common
- Heating or cooling becomes critical

**Subterranean / cavern:**
- Comms relay through structures more difficult
- "Visibility decreases" is more common
- Acoustic events carry differently — echoes confuse direction
- Hull integrity becomes ground integrity — collapse rather than breach

The tables remain functional across settings. Adjust only the texture of how the result is described.

## 13.8 When To Roll

Random Events fire on:

- Mythic's standard event triggers (per Mythic rules)
- Comms attempts that result in a 1 on the d10 oracle (per 3.4)
- Hot-wire backfires (per 10.5) — though backfire has its own table; events from this chapter can supplement
- Any other manual-specified Random Event triggers
- The Warden, or solo player, may also choose to roll an event when a scene needs complication

Do not roll Random Events too frequently. If they fire every scene, they cease to feel random and become routine. Aim for roughly one event per two to four scenes in normal operation; more often as Chaos rises.

The character cannot avoid Random Events by careful play. They can only respond to them. This is part of the manual's pressure — events keep coming, and the resources to deal with them keep shrinking.
